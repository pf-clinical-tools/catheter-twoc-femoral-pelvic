Peri-operative Catheterisation and TWOC Decision Aid (Femoral and Pelvic Fracture Patients)

Nursing decision support tool. Use alongside local policy and clinical judgement. Escalate to a senior doctor/practitioner or urology where uncertainty, complications, repeated failure, macroscopic haematuria, suspected urethral injury, or patient instability occur.

This tool uses pragmatic, orthogeriatric hip/pelvic fracture–focused thresholds intended to standardise bedside decision-making; confirm against local SOP where available.

Caution

  • Do not catheterise without senior input if there is blood visible at the urethral meatus or urethral injury is suspected (particularly with pelvic fractures).
  • If macroscopic haematuria occurs during/after catheterisation, stop and seek senior doctor/practitioner or urology input.
  • If the patient is on therapeutic anticoagulation or has known coagulopathy, seek senior review before catheterisation.

STOP rule

Do NOT proceed with catheterisation if:

  • Significant pain occurs
  • Resistance is encountered
  • The patient becomes distressed
  • The patient becomes clinically unstable

Stop immediately and escalate to a senior doctor/practitioner or urology.
